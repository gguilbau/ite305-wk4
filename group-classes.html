Group Classes

Group classes are the most widely used method of training handlers. Note the terminology: the education that takes place in the classroom is focused on showing you, the handler, how to communicate and interact with your dog in a way that helps both of you. Dog training is a continuous activity that takes place throughout your daily interactions with your pet. We offer a variety of courses that will teach you how you can train your dog to behave in various settings, perform fun tricks, or compete as a canine athlete.

Puppy Class

Puppy classes teach the basics of socialization, household behavior, and beginner techniques such as the use of clicker training and marker words to establish a system of communication. Dogs are eligible until the age of 6 months and must have all appropriate vaccinations for their age to attend. Owners of older dogs looking for beginner lessons should consider obedience level 1.

Obedience

We offer four levels of obedience:

  1. Basic Manners: You will learn how to teach your dog to behave around the house. This training includes heading off common issues such as counter-grazing or rude greetings as well as the introduction of basic action cues (commands).
  2. Around the Shops: You'll practice desensitizing your dog to abrupt stimulii and other dogs, including those who are aggressive. You will learn how to manage leash behavior and prepare your dog to accompany you through public areas without being a nuisance. We will also prepare you for risky or dangerous situations you may encounter while out and about with proven solutions.
  3. Off-leash Conduct: This class focuses on creating a foolproof recall, establishing the rules of the yard, and ways to ensure you're still the most exciting thing in the area even when your dog is off-leash. Dogs should not be left unsupervised while off-leash, but the correct training will help you get more enjoyment with your dog in your own yard or other comparatively safe places.
  4. Competition Obedience: Here, you'll polish various skills such as heel work while preparing your dog to compete in an AKC Obedience Trial. Members who do not aspire to compete are still welcome, but should understand the focus of the class is on scoring rather than simple conditioning.

Agility

Agility classes are offered as a way to explore whether your dog enjoys agility and try out the basics and equipment. Each class meet will focus on a different technique or obstacle in an ongoing rotation. Those looking to compete in sanctioned events may wish to reach out about private instruction so they can focus more effort on the specific skills their dog needs the most practice in and get 1-on-1 feedback.

Trick Training

Trick classes are very open-ended! We will work with attendees to teach dogs common tricks such as play dead or sit pretty. Advanced learners can bring in examples of tricks they may have seen on Instagram or Tiktok and we'll break down how they work together.
